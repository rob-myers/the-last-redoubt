Fixing $\lambda$ we have a quadratic in $t$ whose solutions take the form $(-b ± \sqrt{inSqrt}) / 2a$ where:
- $a := u^2$
- $b := k_2 . \lambda + k_3$
- $c := k_1 . \lambda^2 + k_4 . \lambda + k_5$
- $inSqrt := b^2 - 4ac$

Given $inSqrt < 0$  then there are no solutions and the NPC does not collide.<QedIcon/>
If $inSqrt = 0$ there is exactly one solution
－ a "glancing collision" －
and we may assume the NPC does not collide.<QedIcon/>
Otherwise there are exactly two solutions.

__STRATEGY__
1. Consider both cases $-b \pm \sqrt{inSqrt}$.
1. $inSqrt \geq 0$ provides a quadratic inequality in $\lambda$
   - if $inSqrt(0) \geq 0$ then positive for all $\lambda \in [0, |b_1 - a_1]]$.
   - else can compute zeros and seek one in interval (needn't seek minimal).
1. If find a solution we collide at given time (one of two times), else we don't.

$$
(k_2^2 - 4u^2 k_1) .\lambda^2 +
(2 k_2 k_3  - 4u^2 k_4) . \lambda +
(k_3^2 - 4u^2 k_5)
\geq 0
$$
