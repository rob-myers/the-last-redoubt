Then fixing $i \in I$ consider the equation:
> $p_i(t, \lambda_i) = p(\lambda)$.

Projecting yields two equations:
1. $\alpha_{ix} + \lambda_i \tau_{ix} + tu \delta_x = \alpha_x + \lambda \tau_x$
1. $\alpha_{iy} + \lambda_i \tau_{iy} + tu \delta_y = \alpha_y + \lambda \tau_y$



- Suppose $\tau_{ix} = 0$. Then (1) provides a linear relationship $u \delta_x t - \tau_x \lambda + (\alpha_{ix} - \alpha_{x}) = 0$.
If $\delta_x = \tau_x = 0$ then the NPC and both line segments have direction $(0, \pm 1)$, in which case they do not collide (ignoring coincidence).<QedIcon/>
Otherwise $\delta_x$ or $\tau_x$ are non-zero.
Then they both are non-zero, for otherwise $t$ or $\lambda$ would be constant (these parameters vary over the reals).
Dividing we obtain an equation:
> $t = \frac{\tau_x}{u \delta_x} \lambda + \frac{\alpha_x - \alpha_{ix}}{u \delta_x}$
where $\tau_x \neq 0$.

- Otherwise $\tau_{ix} \neq 0$ in (1); dividing by it and substituting $\lambda_i = ...$ into (2) yields:
  - $a t + b \lambda + c = 0$.
  - $a := u \delta_y - u \delta_x / \tau_{ix}$
  - $b := \tau_x / \tau_{ix} -\tau_y$
  - $c := (\alpha_x - \alpha_{ix}) / \tau_{ix} + (\alpha_{iy} - \alpha_y)$
